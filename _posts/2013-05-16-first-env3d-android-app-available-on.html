--- layout: post title: First env3d android app available on the google play store! date: '2013-05-16T19:38:00.002-07:00' author: Jason Madar tags: modified_time: '2013-05-16T19:56:02.924-07:00' thumbnail: http://1.bp.blogspot.com/-kn2D0y8p5Uc/UZWX7h72vcI/AAAAAAAAAM8/anMBDYnA9zI/s72-c/Screen+Shot+2013-05-16+at+6.19.41+PM.png blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-8298481343865845891.post-2153425774427293909 blogger_orig_url: http://env3d.blogspot.com/2013/05/first-env3d-android-app-available-on.html --- After months of hard work testing the android deployment option, we are thrilled to release our first env3d demo app on the Google play store!  It is a free app and you can get it at https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.vandrico.demo1.  We want to thank Vandrico Solutions for sponsoring this activity, as well as the on going hosting of env3d website.

Of course this is only a demo app, but proves that it is now possible to go from 0 to app store in less than 5 minutes!  The only limit is your imagination!  To create an apk file that you can submit to the app store, simply select the “Create Android Package (Release)” option from the “Env3D Deploy Menu”.


Another major update is that the Android deploy options are now available for the NetBeans plugin.  So user can choose to work in either BlueJ or NetBeans environments.
